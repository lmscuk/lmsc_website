Course overview

A Level Mathematics is the gold standard qualification for students planning to study quantitative subjects at university. It develops deep understanding of pure mathematics, statistics and mechanics, preparing students for degrees in mathematics, engineering, economics, computer science and the physical sciences.

At LMSC, we follow the full British A Level curriculum, taught by specialist mathematicians who understand both the content and the exam requirements. The course is rigorous, structured and designed to build confidence in problem-solving.

This qualification is recognized by universities across the UK and internationally, and is particularly valued for STEM degree entry.

Rigorous content: Full British A Level curriculum covering pure maths, statistics and mechanics

What you'll study

The A Level Mathematics curriculum covers three main areas

Pure Mathematics

  • • Algebra and functions
  • • Coordinate geometry
  • • Sequences and series
  • • Trigonometry
  • • Exponentials and logarithms
  • • Differentiation
  • • Integration
  • • Numerical methods
  • • Vectors

Statistics

  • • Statistical sampling
  • • Data presentation and interpretation
  • • Probability
  • • Statistical distributions
  • • Statistical hypothesis testing
  • • Correlation and regression

Mechanics

  • • Quantities and units in mechanics
  • • Kinematics
  • • Forces and Newton's laws
  • • Moments
  • • Projectiles
  • • Application of mathematical techniques

Assessment & exams

Final Examinations

A Level Mathematics is assessed through three exam papers at the end of Year 13:

  • Paper 1: Pure Mathematics (2 hours)
  • Paper 2: Pure Mathematics (2 hours)
  • Paper 3: Statistics & Mechanics (2 hours)

Internal Assessment

Throughout the course, students complete regular topic tests, termly assessments, and full mock exams to prepare thoroughly for final examinations.

Entry requirements

UK Students

  • Minimum of 5 GCSEs at grade 4/C or above
  • Grade 7 or above in GCSE Mathematics
  • Grade 5 or above in GCSE English Language

International Students

  • Equivalent qualifications to UK GCSEs
  • Strong performance in Mathematics
  • English language proficiency (if applicable)

We assess each application individually. If you're unsure whether you meet the requirements, please get in touch.
