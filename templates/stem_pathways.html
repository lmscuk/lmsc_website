STEM A Level & GCSE Pathways

At London Maths & Science College (LMSC), students follow a structured British curriculum with clear STEM-focused pathways from GCSE to A Level and beyond. We help you choose the right subjects, understand the British system and build a programme that supports progression to UK and global universities.

What do we mean by STEM pathways?

"STEM pathways" at LMSC are structured routes through GCSEs and A Levels (and selected BTEC programmes) that keep doors open to degrees and careers in Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics and related fields.

For many students, the challenge is not just choosing individual subjects, but understanding how those choices fit together over time. We help you plan a coherent pathway — from pre-16 or GCSE level, into A Level or Level 3 study, and onwards to university or professional training.

Our approach helps you:

  • Align subject choices with future degree goals
  • Balance ambition with realistic, sustainable workload
  • Understand typical British entry requirements for STEM degrees

GCSE curriculum and pre-16 preparation

For students who are still working towards GCSEs or need to improve prior results, we offer routes that prepare you for STEM-focused A Level study. Our goal is to ensure you have secure foundations in the core subjects that most British A Level programmes — and universities — expect.

Core GCSEs for STEM

  • • Mathematics
  • • Combined Science or separate Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics)
  • • English Language (and sometimes English Literature)

These subjects are essential for most STEM pathways.

Optional GCSE subjects

Depending on your situation, you might also study subjects such as Computer Science, Business or a language. We'll help you decide which options add value for your future goals.

GCSE retake and booster programmes

For students who need to improve grades in key GCSEs (especially Maths, English and Science), we offer focused support and structured revision programmes, including online options.

A Level STEM curriculum

Our A Level curriculum is built around core STEM subjects and related disciplines that are highly valued by British universities. Most students choose three A Levels, and we help you put together combinations that support your ambitions.

Typical STEM A Level subjects at LMSC include:

• Mathematics
• Further Mathematics
• Physics
• Chemistry
• Biology
• Computer Science
• Economics
• Business

Examples of A Level combinations for:

Engineering

From GCSE to A Level to university

We help students see the bigger picture: how today's choices feed into tomorrow's options.

1

GCSE foundations

Secure strong grades in Maths, Sciences and English, with targeted support or retakes if needed.

2

Choose a STEM A Level pathway

Select three A Levels (and/or a Level 3 BTEC) that align with your interests and typical entry requirements for your target degrees.

3

Two years of focused study

Follow the full British curriculum with regular assessments, mock exams and guidance on study habits and progression.

4

University and beyond

Use your British qualifications to apply to UK universities, international universities or professional programmes, with support on applications and decision-making.

Guidance on choosing the right subjects

Choosing GCSEs and A Levels can feel overwhelming, especially if you're based outside the UK or new to the British system. At LMSC, we offer individual guidance to help you.

Match subjects to degree goals

Connect your subject choices to specific degree goals like Medicine, Engineering, or Computer Science with expert guidance.

Essential vs. preferred subjects

Understand which subjects are essential, preferred or simply useful for different university pathways.

Balanced workload

Balance your choices so that you are stretched but not overloaded, maintaining motivation and wellbeing.

Alternative routes

Plan for alternative routes if your interests or results change, keeping options open for your future.

We speak with both students and families, explaining British entry requirements in clear terms so you can make confident decisions.

Support for international students and families

Many of our students live outside the UK but want the structure and recognition of a British curriculum. We help international families understand:

  • How British GCSEs and A Levels fit into the wider global education landscape
  • How British grades and subjects are viewed by UK and international universities
  • How time zones, exam entries and documentation work in practice

How do my current grades translate?

We review your reports and explain the closest British equivalent, ensuring clear understanding.

Can I mix online and on-campus study?

Yes, we offer fully online and hybrid pathways as well as in-person study in London.

Who helps with university applications?

Our team advises on UCAS, personal statements and references throughout the application process.
