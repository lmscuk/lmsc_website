Fees & Finance

At London Maths & Science College, we aim to be clear and transparent about costs. Here you'll find an overview of our tuition fees, what they include, payment options and support available for UK and international families.

Clear, transparent fees for a British sixth form education

Studying at LMSC is an investment in a recognised British qualification and in your child's future. Our fees reflect small class sizes, specialist teaching and the support needed for students to succeed in demanding A Level and Level 3 BTEC programmes.

We publish our fees openly and explain what is included, any additional costs, and the payment options available. If you are based outside the UK, we can also advise on international payments and typical extra costs (such as exam centre fees in your own country).

Tuition fees are set per academic year

Our fee structure

Fees may vary depending on your programme, study mode and number of subjects. The details below give a typical structure; for exact figures, please refer to our downloadable fee schedule or speak to admissions.

A Level Programmes

  • • Tuition fee per academic year (per 3 A Level package)
  • • Additional fee if adding a 4th subject (e.g. Further Maths)
  • • Different rates for: on-campus, online, hybrid

BTEC & Vocational Pathways

  • • Tuition fee per academic year for full-time Level 3 BTEC
  • • Options for combined A Level + BTEC programmes

GCSE & Retake Programmes

  • • Tuition fee for GCSE packages (full year or intensive)
  • • Retake/improvement programmes for key subjects

Additional Services

  • • Exam entry charges (where not included)
  • • One-off enrolment/registration fee
  • • Optional extras (e.g. additional tutoring)

What your tuition fees include

We want you to be confident about what you are paying for. Unless otherwise stated, tuition fees at LMSC typically include:

Teaching and learning

  • • Live lessons delivered by specialist teachers
  • • Access to lesson recordings (for online/hybrid programmes)
  • • Schemes of work, resources and homework setting

Assessment and reporting

  • • Regular quizzes, topic tests and mock exams
  • • Marking and feedback on assessed work
  • • Written reports and progress reviews

Pastoral and academic support

  • • Contact with a named member of staff
  • • Study skills and exam preparation guidance

Guidance and progression support

  • • Advice on subject choices and pathways
  • • Support with UCAS and university applications
  • • References provided for further study

Additional costs and exam fees

Some costs sit outside core tuition fees. We will always explain these clearly and in advance.

Examination fees +

• Charges for A Level, GCSE or BTEC exam entries

• Additional charges for late entries, resits or private candidate entries at external centres

Textbooks and materials +

• Recommended textbooks or workbooks (where not provided digitally)

• Stationery and scientific calculators

Specialist resources +

For certain subjects, such as lab-based sciences or computing, there may be recommended equipment or software

Optional extras +

Enrichment activities, trips or additional tutoring (if offered)

Note: For international students, we will discuss likely local exam centre fees if you sit exams outside the UK.

Payment options

We understand that families have different financial circumstances and preferences. Subject to our terms and conditions, we typically offer a range of payment options.

Annual payment

Full-fee payment before the start of the academic year. May include a small discount if paid by a set date.

Termly installments

Fees divided into termly payments due at specified points in the year.

Monthly installments

Regular monthly payments via direct debit or agreed payment plan.

You will receive a clear schedule of due dates and amounts when you enrol.

Deposits, refunds and withdrawals

These details will be set out in your enrolment contract, but the key principles should be clear on the website.

Deposits

A non-refundable deposit may be required to secure a place. This deposit is typically offset against the first instalment of tuition fees.

Cancellation before the course starts

We outline deadlines by which you can cancel without being liable for full fees (apart from any non-refundable deposit).

Withdrawal during the year

Our terms and conditions explain how much notice is required and what proportion of fees remain payable if a student withdraws mid-year.

Changes of programme or study mode

Changes (e.g. from full-time to reduced load, or in-person to online) may affect fees. Any changes will be discussed and confirmed in writing.

Scholarships, bursaries and financial support

Where possible, LMSC aims to ensure that financial circumstances are not the only barrier to a student accessing our programmes. We may offer a limited number of scholarships or bursaries, usually based on academic potential and/or financial need.

Availability and criteria vary from year to year. Please speak to our admissions team if you would like to discuss possible support or to see the current policy.

International payments

For families based outside the UK, we can accept international payments via bank transfer (and, if you wish, card payments or third-party platforms).

  • All fees are set and invoiced in GBP (£)
  • Any bank charges or currency conversion fees are normally the responsibility of the payer
  • We provide clear payment instructions and due dates in your offer letter

If you have questions about international payments, our admissions and finance teams can provide guidance.
